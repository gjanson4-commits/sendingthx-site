SendingThx is operated by SendingThx LLC, a Wyoming limited liability company. These Terms govern all SMS and MMS messages sent under the SendingThx name, including the Thx, Ping, Alert, and Msg notification types described below.

What these messages are

Every message sent through SendingThx is a transactional delivery notification — triggered by a confirmed delivery event from a carrier or fulfillment partner, equivalent to a standard carrier delivery alert. Messages let you know a package has arrived, confirm that delivery to whoever sent it, and — where applicable — carry a reply back to the sender.

SendingThx does not send marketing, promotional, or advertising messages of any kind under this program. No message contains discount codes, promotions, or unrelated offers.

How you came to receive a message

If you received a SendingThx text, it's because a purchaser made a real purchase from one of our retail partners and chose, themselves, to send you a one-time delivery notification. This is a Consumer-Initiated Viral Message: the purchaser — not SendingThx — manually enters your phone number and must affirmatively confirm they have your consent to receive this single notification before it can be sent. SendingThx does not import contact lists, buy phone numbers, or message anyone the purchaser hasn't specifically identified and personally vouched for.

In plain terms: SendingThx doesn't collect phone numbers or build a contact list. Every number used comes from a single purchaser, entered by hand, for one specific delivery — and that purchaser has affirmatively confirmed they have your consent before anything is sent.

How consent is collected

Purchaser affirmatively confirms recipient consent. At checkout, the purchaser enters the recipient's phone number themselves and must check an unchecked-by-default box confirming they have that recipient's consent to receive this one-time delivery notification from SendingThx. This confirmation is specific to that recipient and that order — it is not a store-wide setting, and no message is sent unless the purchaser affirmatively checks it for that transaction.

Purchaser affirmatively confirms recipient consent — shown on the participating retailer's storefront, before checkout

Default state — unchecked

After the purchaser confirms

This cart-time confirmation appears under the SendingGifts name — a SendingThx service focused on gift delivery notifications and consent collection.

Example message

Recipient receives: "Your birthday flowers from Green Garden Florist just arrived — sent by Bob via SendingThx. One-time notification sent at Bob's request. Reply STOP to opt out." Recipient can reply with a quick thank-you — relayed privately back to Bob. No app, no login, no sign-up required.

Gifter's text thread: "Your birthday flowers from Green Garden Florist were just delivered to Mom. Reply STOP to opt out." — sent from SendingThx, the same sender name used across Thx, Ping, Alert, and Msg.

Message types

  • Thx — lets a gift recipient send a one-tap thank-you, with an optional photo, back to whoever sent the gift.
  • Ping — a simultaneous delivery notification sent to both the recipient and the gifter when a gift arrives.
  • Alert — a general delivery notification for any order, sent to the purchaser and up to two additional designated recipients.
  • Msg — an optional personal note from the sender, attached to a Ping or Alert notification.

Message frequency

Message frequency depends entirely on your delivery activity. A typical order generates one to three messages: a delivery alert, and — if you choose to respond — a confirmation that your reply was relayed. SendingThx does not send recurring, scheduled, or unprompted messages outside of an actual delivery event.

Message and data rates

Message and data rates may apply, depending on your mobile carrier and plan.

Opt-out and help

Reply STOP to any message at any time to stop receiving further messages tied to that delivery. For help with a message you received, email us directly at support@sendingthx.com rather than replying to the text.

Note: because each message is tied to a single delivery rather than a standing subscription, there's generally no ongoing list to opt back into — a new delivery from a participating retailer would generate a new, independent notification.

Privacy

Phone numbers used by SendingThx are provided directly by the retailer as part of a verified order and are used only to deliver the notifications described above. Where a message is relayed between a recipient and a sender (as with Thx and Alert), SendingThx acts as a privacy-preserving intermediary — the recipient and sender are not shown each other's personal phone numbers.

Carriers

Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages. SendingThx works to ensure timely delivery but cannot guarantee message delivery in all circumstances.
